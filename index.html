Recent Projects

Udacity - Self-Driving Car Nanodegree Project 1

PID Controller to Maneuver the Vehicle around the Track

Implemented a PID controller in C++ to compute the vehicle's appropriate steering angle to safely drive around the virtual track while keeping the vehicle center of the road.

Udacity - Self-Driving Car Nanodegree Project 2

Kidnapped Vehicle

Implemented a 2 dimensional particle filter in C++. Observed the behaviour of the vehicle driving along with all of its landmark measuremnts in the simulator. The map and some initial localization information (GPS) was provided.

Udacity - Self-Driving Car Nanodegree Project 3

Extended Kalman Filter

Implemented the extended kalman filter in C++ to detect bicycle that traveled around the vehicle. Used a Kalman filter, lidar measurements, and radar measurments to track the bicycle's position and velocity.

Udacity - Self-Driving Car Nanodegree Project 4

Behavioral Cloning

Built and trained a convolution neural network (CNN) in Keras to predict the vehicle's steering angles. I collected the data by driving the car in a simulator in a forward direction for three laps, and then in the opposite direction for another two laps. I used the OpenCV library to load the images and cropped the images 70px from the top and 25 px from the bottom to remove unwanted pixels. The data were split 80:20, meaning 80% training and 20% validation. The model architecture was based on NVIDIA's CNN. The model included RELU layers to introduce nonlinearity and used the Keras lambda layer to normalize the data. The model contained a dropout layer to reduce overfitting and used an adam optimizer.

Udacity - Self-Driving Car Nanodegree Project 5

Traffic Sign Classifier

Built and trained a CNN model based on LeNet architecture to classify German traffic signs. The images were converted to grayscale to reduce the number of features, thus, reducing execution time, which achieved a final accuracy of 94.1%. The model was developed using Python and TensorFlow.

Udacity - Self-Driving Car Nanodegree Project 6

Advanced Lane Finding

Built a pipeline in Python to identify the lane boundaries in a video from a front-facing camera on a car. The libraries/dependencies that were used in this project are OpenCV, Matplotlib, and Numpy. The first step involved computing the camera calibration matrix and distortion coefficients using chessboard images. After obtaining the matrices, the distortion correction was applied to raw images. The second step involved creating a thresholded binary image using colour transform and gradients. The third step involved converting an image to Perspective Transform (Bird-eye view). The fourth step involved plotting a histogram of the bird-eye view binary image to decide which pixels belong to the left line and which belong to the right line. The fifth step involved implementing a sliding window algorithm to find and follow the lane lines up to the frame's top and then fit a polynomial. The final step involved measuring the curvature of the line lines.
